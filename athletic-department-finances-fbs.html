How Much Money Does Each College Really Have

There are truly two sides to this argument. The top departments make millions of dollars in revenue each year, and yet there are many colleges that not only lack profit but run a defecit. The U.S. Department of Education runs the Equity in Athletics Data Analysis which is in charge of collecting and distributing data from federally funded universities. While all the data comes directly from the schools, there is one major point of contention with the data. In the collection of data, the program does not allow athletics to run a defecit following the assumption that the department is getting funding from somewhere and, therefore, not actually running a defecit. As a result, it is likely that a majority of schools that have a profit of $0 are actually running a deficit.
